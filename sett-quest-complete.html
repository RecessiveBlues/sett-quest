Case Study: Maya

Maya is a 3rd grade student with dyslexia who struggles with reading tasks. She's enthusiastic about science but gets frustrated during independent reading activities.

Student Information

Maya has dyslexia, reads at a 1st grade level, loves science, gets frustrated easily, and has strong verbal skills.

Environment Information

General education classroom with 24 students. Reading center has print books. Computer lab available twice weekly.

Tasks Information

Independent reading of science texts, writing lab reports, participating in group discussions.

Environment Information

Middle school with 6 different classrooms daily. Most assignments require handwritten work. 1:1 laptop program in the school.

Key SETT Model Takeaways

  • Student: Consider the whole student - strengths, challenges, interests, and preferences. Go beyond focusing on just the disability.
  • Environment: Analyze all settings where the student needs to function, not just a single location. Technology should work across all relevant environments.
  • Tasks: Break down specific task demands to understand exactly where barriers exist. Different tasks may require different accommodations.
  • Tools: Select technologies that address the specific needs identified in your analysis. The right tool bridges the gap between student abilities and task demands.
  • Integration: The most effective solutions consider how all four components interconnect. No single component should be evaluated in isolation.

By applying the SETT model systematically, you can make more effective assistive technology recommendations that truly meet students' needs in all contexts.
